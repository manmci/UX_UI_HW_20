About the Organization

Edúcate Ya is a non-profit organization in Portland, OR that focuses on helping the Latino Community by offering English Classes, information about STI prevention, social events, and more.

Founder Rafael Arellano created the organization in 2000 after he noticed a lack of Latinos in leadership roles. Since then, Edúcate Ya has worked to educate Latinos in how to navigate the Western system to achieve better jobs and access to healthcare.

Project Overview

This was a group design project. I worked with two other designers from my bootcamp. While the entire process was collaborative, I focused on user interviews, interaction design, color design, wireframing, prototyping, and illustration. My team worked in Figma, Procreate, and Google Slides.

We had three weeks (30 hours of in-class time) to take this design from a blank page to a fully responsive prototype, and if you ask me, we nailed it.

User Reasearch

As a team, we conducted an interview with the founder of Edúcate Ya, as well as their social media manager, who oversaw the website as well.

I conducted four potential user interviews over social media. 3 of 4 interviewees were from the Latin American community, with origins from different cultures.

Using insights from the interviews, we defined our proto persona, Oscar, with an Empathy Map to get a better idea of his throughts, feelings, needs, and goals.

Definition

Taking a mobile-first design approach, my teammates and I each individually did mobile wireframe sketches, then combined aspects of our ideas into a single wireframe/lo-fi prototype.

Ideation

To explore how to best design for users like Oscar, and create a website that would better aid Edúcate Ya of achieving its mission, we created a storyboard. This was a collaborative illustration project with my teammate, Carlos. We took turns drawing panels, and I inked and added finishing touches.
